(11) Decision: Endorse the People's Petition for Peace (5 min) *Sponsor*: Sadie Fulton *Background and Purpose*: The People's Petition for Peace was launched by a peace coalition after a delegation was sent to South Korea. The Green Party of Korea has been leading the charge in pushing for the abolition of THAAD, the "missile-defense system" pointed straight over Seoul, and that the anti-THAAD movement has managed to have one president successfully impeached. Now, there is a desperate need to block military escalations between the White House and North Korea, and the people of South Korea are looking to Greens in America for help. Jill Stein was a member of the South Korean delegation, and has done an excellent job taking a lead role on building the People's Petition for Peace as well as highlighting the Green Party's involvement, positioning us as a leader in the anti-war movement. These efforts should be supported, encouraged, and formally endorsed. *Proposal*: That the Green Party of California endorse the People's Petition for Peace, starting with a formal endorsement by the CC. *Implementation/Timeline/Resources*: **NOTES** Sadie stated that Jill Stein has been pushing a Petition for Peace and she feels that GPCA should support this. Pased by consensus.

(12) Decision: CC Action to Assist in Implementation of State Party Functions (10 min) *Sponsor*: Mica Daniel, Alameda County *Background and Purpose*: Throughout the calendar year, a variety of efforts have been pursued by the CC to implement the functioning of the State Party despite a centralized group of Green Party members refusing to cooperate in the implementation of State Party functions. Some of these Greens - whether members of the CC or not "” have effectively hamstrung the State Party in its efforts to continue to implement necessary Party functions, such as the administration of Standing General Assembly voting, financial management, welcoming new Greens, and other functions. The CC recently authorized the CC Co-Coordinators to pursue legal advice in anticipation of securing control of GPCA "assets." The CC also recently took action at its October 2017 meeting regarding: (1) the transfer of two PayPal accounts to the control of the elected Treasurer, (2) the transfer of GPCA funds currently in the One United GPCA bank accounts in Los Angeles to the Travis Credit Union account, and to close the One United accounts, (3) the appointment of Finance Committee Co-Coordinator Victoria Ashley as a person authorized to sign checks and/or authorize them via the GPCA bank account if/when the Treasurer is not available; and (4) the removal of all existing members who currently sign checks and/or authorize them via the GPCA bank account if/when the Treasurer is not available. In the meantime, the Co-Coordinators have sought and obtained legal advice to help in these efforts and, as a result of that advice, are making the following proposal: *Proposal*: That the Coordinating Committee affirm and reaffirm the following findings - 1. With respect to the State's funds that are kept in the One United Bank, (One United Bank Corporate Office: 3683 Crenshaw Blvd. Los Angeles, CA, 90016), it is the Coordinating Committee's intent that all funds be under the control of the State Party Treasurer, Ruscal Cayangyang and that the Treasurer be authorized to communicate with One United Bank as the Party's authorized financial representative; AND 2. With respect to any accounts opened in the Green Party of California's name with PayPal or other credit agencies or services, it is the Coordinating Committee's intent that any credit or charges to these accounts on behalf of the GPCA be under the control of the State Party Treasurer, Ruscal Cayangyang and that the Treasurer be authorized to communicate with such creditor(s) as the Party's authorized financial representative; AND 3. Also with respect to any accounts opened in the Green Party of California's name with PayPal or other credit agencies or services, it is the Coordinating Committee's intent that the State Party Treasurer, Ruscal Cayangyang be solely authorized to make charges or incur debts on behalf of the GPCA associated with such creditor(s); AND 4. With respect to other services for which the GPCA is charged recurring, intermittent or other charges or fees, such as NationBuilder, it is the Coordinating Committee's intent that the State Party Treasurer, Ruscal Cayangyang be solely authorized to communicate with such services regarding the authorization for any charges or invoicing for services on behalf of the GPCA; AND 5. It is the Coordinating Committee's intent that the GPCA Treasurer provide a financial update to the next General Assembly and include recommendations regarding budgetary and other financial matters. The report should also include an update on the status of the efforts to implement the foregoing items; AND 6. It is the Coordinating Committee's intent to continue to authorize the Co-Coordinators, Bert Heuer and Mica Daniel, and Alternate Co-Coordinator, Victoria Ashley, to communicate with legal advisor(s) regarding how best to ensure State Party assets are maintained appropriately and to report back to the Coordinating Committee periodically as these efforts proceed. *Implementation*: Treasurer, Finance Committee, Co-Coordinators, Alt.
